Reading

The compulsory reading (as found on the schedule) should be done before class. The recommended follow-up reading is non-compulsory and is to supplement your learning. I recommend completing at least one follow-up reading per week.

Baranowski, Maciej. 2017. “Class Matters: The Sociolinguistics of Goose and Goat in Manchester English.” Language Variation and Change 29 (3). Cambridge University Press: 301–39. doi:10.1017/S0954394517000217.

Baranowski, Maciej, and Danielle Turton. 2018. “The FOOT-STRUT vowels in Manchester: Evidence for the Diachronic Precursor to the Split?” University of Pennsylvania Working Papers in Linguistics 24 (2).

Bermúdez-Otero, Ricardo. 2007. “Word-Final Prevocalic Consonants in English: Representation Vs Derivation.” Paper presented at the Old World Conference in Phonology 4, Rhodes. Handout available at http://www.bermudez-otero.com/OCP4.pdf.

Docherty, Gerard J, and Paul Foulkes. 2005. “Glottal Variants of /t/ in the Tyneside variety of English.” A Figure of Speech: A Festschrift for John Laver, 173–97.

Evans, Bronwen G, and Paul Iverson. 2007. “Plasticity in Vowel Perception and Production: A Study of Accent Change in Young Adults.” The Journal of the Acoustical Society of America 121 (6). Acoustical Society of America: 3814–26.

Foulkes, Paul. 1997. “Rule Inversion in a British English Dialect: A Sociolinguistic Investigation of [r]-Sandhi in Newcastle Upon Tyne.” University of Pennsylvania Working Papers in Linguistics 4 (1): 16.

Johnson, Keith. 2012. Acoustic and Auditory Phonetics. 3rd ed.. Malden, MA: Wiley-Blackwell.

Labov, William. 1994. Principles of Linguistic Change: Internal Factors. Oxford, U.K.: Blackwell.

Ladefoged, Peter. 1996. Elements of Acoustic Phonetics. 2nd ed.. Chicago: University of Chicago Press.

Ladefoged, Peter, and Keith Johnson. 2015. A Course in Phonetics. 7th ed.. Stamford, Conn.: Cengage Learning. https://libsearch.ncl.ac.uk/primo-explore/fulldisplay?docid=NCL_ALMA51104441400002411&context=L&vid=NEWUI&search_scope=NotPC&tab=default_tab&lang=en_US.

Lippi-Green, Rosina. 2012. English with an Accent: Language, Ideology and Discrimination in the United States. Routledge.

McMahon, April MS. 1994. Understanding Language Change. Cambridge University Press.

Piercy, Caroline. 2012. “A Transatlantic Cross-Dialectal Comparison of Non-Prevocalic /r/.” University of Pennsylvania Working Papers in Linguistics 18 (2): 10.

Queen, Robin. 2015. Vox Popular: The Surprising Life of Language in the Media. John Wiley & Sons.

Sankoff, Gillian. 2004. “Adolescents, Young Adults and the Critical Period: Two Case Studies from ‘Seven up’.” Sociolinguistic Variation: Critical Reflections, 121–39.

Thomas, Erik R. 2011. Sociophonetics : An Introduction. New York: Palgrave Macmillan.

Turton, Danielle. 2014. “Some /l/S Are Darker Than Others: Accounting for Variation in English /l/ with Ultrasound Tongue Imaging.” Penn Working Papers in Linguistics 20 (2).

Wells, J. C. 1982. Accents of English. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press.
